Not a counselor, but I assisted the head of the post baccalaureate program to help students get to med school. A prospective student sent in his application for the fall semester.

His essay was all about his experience being on the list for a new heart. He had been on this list for years. He talked about feeling like giving up at points, but he knew he would get there. He wanted to become a doctor so that he could help others.

It was a great essay. He was accepted into the program. He later called and asked if he could delay his acceptance to the spring semester because he was getting a new heart!

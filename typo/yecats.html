Former admissions counselor here.

A student wrote about his sister as an inspiration, because she was only 4'11 and unlikely to lead a normal life. As a fellow shorty, this was eye-opening to me.

A "Why I want to go to your college" essay that compared an potential acceptance to consummating a relationship with a lover. Ew.

The most memorable essays were often about very simple moments in life, or relationships with parents/siblings/friends. They were memorable for being well-written, honest and authentic. In general, satire doesn't usually work. Sometimes it goes very, very badly.
